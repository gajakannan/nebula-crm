# Prioritization Frameworks

Use these frameworks selectively. Choose based on decision type, not preference.

## 1) Now/Next/Later

### Use when
- Communicating roadmap direction.
- Aligning stakeholders on sequence without false precision.

### Output shape
- `Now`: active delivery focus.
- `Next`: prepared and likely after current work.
- `Later`: valid but intentionally deferred.

### Pitfalls
- Overfilling `Now`.
- Treating `Later` as a commitment date.

## 2) RICE

### Formula
`RICE score = (Reach * Impact * Confidence) / Effort`

### Use when
- Ranking competing opportunities.
- Comparing mixed initiatives with limited capacity.

### Inputs
- Reach: users/accounts/events per time window.
- Impact: relative value multiplier.
- Confidence: certainty in assumptions (0-100%).
- Effort: team-weeks or person-months.

### Pitfalls
- Inflated reach estimates.
- Ignoring strategic constraints outside score.

## 3) WSJF

### Formula
`WSJF = Cost of Delay / Job Size`

Where Cost of Delay commonly combines:
- Business value
- Time criticality
- Risk reduction or opportunity enablement

### Use when
- Delivery order is constrained by timing and dependencies.
- Delay has clear economic or operational downside.

### Pitfalls
- Scoring with inconsistent scales between teams.
- Using WSJF without dependency mapping.

## 4) MoSCoW

### Categories
- Must have
- Should have
- Could have
- Won't have (this release)

### Use when
- Release scope must be cut or locked.
- Teams need clear inclusion/exclusion boundaries.

### Pitfalls
- Labeling too many items as Must.
- Failing to document why items are Won't.

## 5) Kano

### Categories
- Must-be: expected baseline.
- Performance: more is better.
- Delighter: unexpected value.
- Indifferent: little user value.

### Use when
- Balancing parity features with differentiation.
- Deciding where delight investment is worth it.

### Pitfalls
- Assuming delighters remain delighters over time.
- Ignoring baseline gaps while pursuing novelty.

## Selection Cheat Sheet

- Roadmap communication: `Now/Next/Later`
- Backlog ranking under uncertainty: `RICE`
- Dependency and urgency dominated ordering: `WSJF`
- Fixed-window release cuts: `MoSCoW`
- Baseline vs differentiation choices: `Kano`

## Minimum Quality Bar

Every prioritization output should include:
- Chosen framework(s) and rationale.
- Explicit assumptions.
- Confidence level or uncertainty notes.
- Dependency and risk callouts.
